Interesting Facts about the Chameleon

  • Their tail is prehensile, which means they can easily hang from branches.
  • They use their colour changes as a way of communication.
  • They have almost 360 degree eyesight and can see in opposite directions from both eyes at the same time!

Interesting Facts about the Pigeon

  • Pigeons are very complex and intelligent.
  • They have excellent hearing.
  • They are scared of magnets, as they have a kind of built-in compass which goes haywire when close to magnets.

Interesting Facts about the Camel

  • Most people think the camel's hump holds water, but it actually stores fat.
  • They have 3 sets of eyelids and 2 rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • When they finally find water, they can drink upto as much as 40 gallons!

Interesting Facts about the Brown Bear

  • The brown bear is the largest predator still living on the continent of Europe.
  • They can reach speeds of upto 50 kilometres per hour.
  • In the wild, these omnivores spend up to 16 hours a day looking for food!

Interesting Facts about the Cicada

  • All cicada species in North America came from a common ancestor. However, that ancestor comes from Australia!
  • The body of a cicada is similar to that of a violin, it consists of some empty spaces that act like a hole and amplify the sound.
  • They can occasionally switch from living for 13 years to living for 17 years and vice versa!
